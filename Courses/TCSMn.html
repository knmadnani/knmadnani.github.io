About the Course

The course is evenly split between:

  • Discrete Mathematics (50%)
    • Sets, Functions, Relations, Partial Orders
    • Recurrences, Summations
    • Counting, Generating Functions, Asymptotics
    • Elementary Logic and Proof Techniques
    • Introduction to Graphs
    • Group Theory (if time permits)
    • Reference Book:
      Discrete Mathematics and Its Applications (Indian adaptation by Kamala Krithivasan) – Kenneth. H. Rosen
      No need to buy books, if you don't prefer. I will be giving some notes which should cover the essentials.
  • Formal Languages and Automata Theory (50%):
  • Reference Book: TBD
    • Alphabets, Languages
    • Regular Languages: Finite Automata, Regex
    • Context-Free Languages: PDAs, CFGs
    • Recursively Enumerable Languages: Turing Machines
    • Introduction to Computability and Complexity (if time permits)

Lecture Slides

  • Lectures 1 and 2 : Course Introduction, Grading Schemes, etc. and Introduction to Sets
  • Lecture 3 : Introduction to Relations and Functions
  • Lectures 4 and 5 : Comparing Cardinality of infinite sets, Countability, Introduction to Propositional Logic.
  • Lecture 6 : Propositional Logic Continued.
  • Lectures 7 : Introduction to Predicate Logic.
  • Lectures 8 : Introduction to Algorithms (By Sriram).
  • Lectures 9 : Introduction to Asymptotics (By Sriram).
  • Lectures 10 and 11 : Rules of Inference and Proof Strategies.
  • Lecture 12 : Induction and Recursive Definition.
  • Lecture 13 : Relations-revisiting in detail - Equivalence Relation, Parital Order, Closure, Well Quasi Order, Higman's Lemma, multiset ordering.
